Kairui Bao

MSc Statistics, Oxford · Incoming ML PhD, NTU Singapore

I am a master's student in Statistics at the University of Oxford (Keble College). Before Oxford, I completed an Honours BSc with High Distinction in Mathematics & Statistics at the University of Toronto. In August 2026 I will begin my PhD in Machine Learning at Nanyang Technological University (NTU), Singapore, on a full NTU Research Scholarship.

Broadly speaking, my research sits at the intersection of dynamic networks and statistical machine learning — with a particular focus on data-driven parameter estimation for dynamical systems in high-dimensional regimes. I am interested in machine learning theory, high-dimensional statistics, dynamical systems, and modelling high-dimensional sparse networks. My current work centers on the broad theme of learning high-dimensional time series (or dynamical systems) on dynamical networks. I am also exploring the more engineering side of machine learning, including NLP, LLMs, and reinforcement learning.

I am open to collaboration on machine learning theory (online-learning theory, RL theory, deep-learning theory), and on applied machine-learning problems with theoretical grounding.

Reach me at kairui.bao@keble.ox.ac.uk.

Research Experience

  • Goodness-of-Fit Tests via Kernel Density Estimation

    Jun 2025 – Oct 2025

    Research Assistant · Prof. Luai Al Labadi, University of Toronto

    Developed univariate and multivariate goodness-of-fit tests based on kernel density estimation, with reflection-method boundary correction, bandwidth-matrix selection, and geometric invariance. Proved consistency and asymptotic convergence to the Kullback–Leibler divergence under fixed alternatives, validated through extensive simulations and real-data applications.

  • Advancements in Entropy Estimation for Model Assessment

    Jan 2025 – Jun 2025

    Research Assistant · Prof. Luai Al Labadi, University of Toronto

    Built an entropy-estimation algorithm using m-spacing and Correa's estimators, derived the likelihood-ratio test statistic and its independence properties, and ran power analyses against several alternatives (validated on Yarn Strength and River Flow data).

  • ChatGPT-Driven Education: A Student-Centered Approach

    Sep 2024 – Feb 2025

    Research Assistant · Prof. Luai Al Labadi, University of Toronto

    Designed experiments (Bonferroni / Šidák corrections) and analysed learning outcomes with ANOVA, Mann–Whitney, and t-tests to compare ChatGPT-assisted learning against traditional teaching.

  • 3D Object Detection Using Point Clouds

    May 2024 – Aug 2024

    Research Assistant · Prof. Jun Xiao, Zhejiang University

    Studied point-cloud representations for 3D recognition and applied PointNet-style architectures toward intelligent-driving perception.
